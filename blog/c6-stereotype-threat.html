Stereotype Threat

Let's not make it worse

This week we were instructed to write about Stereotype Threat. In layman’s terms, this is the term that describes when people do worse at a task because they believe their group isn’t supposed to do well at this task. The classic example is that black students perform worse on a standardized test if they are told beforehand that black students typically perform worse.

As a white male, there aren’t a whole lot of negative performance stereotypes that I’m aware of (if there are any, don’t tell me about them). So I couldn’t think of any examples where my white male-ness may have had a detrimental affect on my performance. Something, potentially related, is that there were times during college when I took classes that I wasn’t “supposed” to take. For example, I enrolled in a class that is almost exclusively taken by seniors in their last year. I was a Freshman (with a pretty terrible counselor). I got one of the lowest grades I’ve ever gotten that semester. This poor performance could potentially be blamed on everyone’s belief that I wasn’t supposed to be in that class. However, that was never my attitude. Once I understood the odds were stacked against me I put more time and effort into the class because I wanted desperately to get better grades on each paper and test than my much more learned classmates.

For anyone reading this that has experienced a real stereotype threat, I’m sorry and I hope my previous example doesn’t make you angry. I really just don’t understand what’s it like for others to expect you to fail. I definitely understand that the world of tech has been dominated by white and asian males. For those that don’t fit into either of those categories I understand that this program is an even scarier leap of faith than it is for me. What I need to do is make sure that I don’t take any actions that highlight the stereotype of a male dominated tech culture. I just hope that being aware of stereotype threat doesn’t make me even more aware of the differences in race and gender...
